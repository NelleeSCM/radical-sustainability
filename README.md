# Radical Sustainability

**Planet → People → Profit**

Radical Sustainability is an open **decision‑ordering framework** for use where actions create irreversible or long‑term impacts on ecological systems, human wellbeing, or future generations.

It exists to prevent false trade‑offs, especially those that justify harm on the basis of technical capability, economic efficiency, or short‑term gain.

This repository is an **open commons**.  
No individual or institution owns, governs, or speaks for Radical Sustainability.

---

## Core Principle

All decisions must be evaluated in the following **non‑negotiable order**:

**Planet → People → Profit**

This ordering is hierarchical, not additive.

- **Planet** refers to the integrity of Earth’s life‑support systems, including climate stability, ecosystems, biodiversity, and biophysical thresholds.
- **People** refers to human health, safety, dignity, justice, and intergenerational wellbeing.
- **Profit** refers to economic value, efficiency, growth, or financial return.

Lower tiers must not override higher tiers.

---

## What Radical Sustainability Is

- A **constraint framework**, not an ideology
- A tool for **professional judgment**, not persuasion
- A way to **invalidate certain arguments**, not prescribe outcomes
- Compatible with existing ethical and professional codes

Radical Sustainability does not ask:
> “Is this beneficial overall?”

It asks:
> **“Is this permissible at all?”**

---

## What It Is Not

Radical Sustainability is not:
- A branding exercise
- A political platform
- A certification scheme
- A claim of moral purity
- A replacement for existing laws or professional codes

It is intentionally minimal and incomplete.

---

## Redefinition of Progress

Progress is defined as:

> **The disciplined restraint of capability in service of long‑term planetary and human viability.**

Technical feasibility does not create ethical obligation.  
Economic benefit does not confer moral permission.
